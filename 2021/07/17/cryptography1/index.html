PicoCTF2020 Writeups Part 1

Mod 26

We are given a prompt: “Cryptography can be easy, do you know what ROT13 is?” followed by a short encrypted text: cvpbPGS{arkg_gvzr_V’yy_gel_2_ebhaqf_bs_ebg13_GYpXOHqX}. We simply have to put the text through a Rot13 decoder. This gives us the flag, picoCTF{next_time_I’ll_try_2_rounds_of_rot13_TLcKBUdK}

The Numbers

We are given an image of various numbers:

The hint tells us that the flag is in the format of PICOCTF{} which is different than the previous problems. Looking closely at the image we can see that there are 7 numbers before curly brackets appear. This mirrors the format of PICOCTF{}, using this we can see that the numbers correspond to letters in the alphabet(ex. 16 = p, 9 = i, 3 = c, etc ). This is a basic number substitution cipher and can either be solved by hand or through a A1Z26 decoder, giving us the flag PicoCTF{thenumbersmason}, but remember to capitalize every letter as hinted giving us the final flag PICOCTF{THENUMBERSMASON}.

13

We are given a prompt referencing Rot 13 followed by an encrypted flag: cvpbPGS{abg_gbb_onq_bs_n_ceboyrz}. Putting the encrypted text through a Rot13 decoder, we get the flag: picoCTF{not_too_bad_of_a_problem}

Caesar

We are given an encrypted flag picoCTF{gvswwmrkxlivyfmgsrhnrisegl}, putting this through a Caesar cipher(https://www.dcode.fr/caesar-cipher) we get a list of possible flags, the one that is not random gibberish is most likely the flag in this case the flag is crossingtherubicondjneoach, picoCTF{crossingtherubicondjneoach}.

Pixelated

We are given two images of random pixels, the hints prompt us to an article about Visual cryptography in which they superimpose two pixelated images together to show a message. Using this information we know that we have to find a way to combine the two images. There are various methods to do this including photoshop, but Stegsolve(https://github.com/zardus/ctf-tools/tree/master/stegsolve) is a free option. Launching Stegsolve, click file and then scrambled1.png, then click analyze and image combiner and open scrambled2.png. Click through the various combinations and we reach ADD which gives us the flag picoCTF{0542dc1d}.
